5.3 Navier-Stokes and the Yang-Mills Bracket: The Lamb Form

The key to connecting NS with Yang-Mills is to work with the NS 
MOMENTUM equation in Lamb form, rather than the vorticity equation.

The Navier-Stokes momentum equation can be written as:

  ∂u/∂t + u × ω + ∇Π = νΔu                    (Lamb form)

where ω = curl(u) is the vorticity and Π = p + |u|²/2 is the Bernoulli 
pressure. The nonlinear term u × ω is the Lamb vector, related to the 
standard convective derivative by:

  (u · ∇)u = u × ω + ∇(|u|²/2)

The Yang-Mills equation for the cross-product connection, in temporal 
gauge A₀ = 0, contains the bracket term:

  Σ_j [A^u_j, F^u_{jk}] = Σ_j A^u_j × F^u_{jk}     (via so(3) ≅ ℝ³)

bracket Σ_j [A^u_j, F^u_{jk}] for the cross-product connection

vector (u × ω) and the Bernoulli pressure gradient (∇|u|²/2) through
